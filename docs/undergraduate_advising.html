Undergraduate Advising App

10 Weeks

Challenge

The current undergraduate advising website for the University of Washington only provides booking information for general advisers. Students exploring different majors or students with major-related questions must navigate to the individual webpages of each department to schedule appointments with advisers.

Proposed Solution

After conducting some background research, our team designed and developed a more dedicated undergraduate advising site to allow users to schedule advising appointments with any department from one central location.

Impact

Created an improved and functionable web app for undergraduate students at the University of Washington to explore different majors and schedule advising appointments with ease.

UW Advising App Presentation

Background + Research

The current undergraduate advising website for the University of Washington (UW) provides students with a multitude of resources pertaining to degree requirements, major planning, academic support, and more. The site as a whole includes lots of helpful resources for the purpose of answering questions that undergraduate students might ask during advising appointments. Given the size of the university, this makes sense that these resources would be made available to filter out the number of students scheduling advising appointments, and to answer generic questions. However, the distinction between general undergraduate advising and departmental advising isn't exactly clear.



When students select "BOOK AN IN-PERSON OR ONLINE ADVISING APPOINTMENT", they are taken to a page containing the content above. The number of different links on this page could potentially be overwhelming to a user, and it is misleading because it is not what a student would expect to encounter when clicking on the booking link. A student might expect options to directly book adviser appointments, but this is not the case. There is no central site for students to book advising appointments.

When looking to meet with an adviser, they are taken to this general advising website and may be unaware that major-specific questions can only be answered by departmental advisers. It takes some digging, but you can eventually find a list of all the departments at UW with contact information for the advising offices. Even this resource is unfortunately outdated. The department link only takes you to information in the Student Guide, rather than the official website for appointment booking.

Brainstorming

For brainstorming, we exercised rapid ideation to acquire a diverse list of possible interactive features for the web app. A constraint for this project was the need to design and implement at least two interactive features. Interactive features change the view of the web page in some way based on some sort of dataset. (We planned to use our own .JSON data) .

User Testing

Each team member asked 2-3 people to provide feedback on both the design and usability of our first prototype. Following this, we reiterated upon our first prototype by implementing their feedback. Below are some comments our users gave us:

  • "I don't know how to get to the landing page from the advising page."

  • "It would be nice to automatically filter availability by the slider instead of clicking a button."

  • "The stars for favorites should be filled when selected."

Final Product

Previous Next


Reiterating upon our first prototype using the feedback we received from user testing, our final design used HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and added React.js and Firebase Hosting for data persistence. Some notable differences between our first prototype and final design was the navigation bar, which allowed for clearer organization of the website, and the overall improvement of our interactive features. We also integrated our web app with Firebase Hosting, so users could log in and save their data. The data saved upon logging out were the different departments each user favorited.
