Google Authenticator Redesign

10 Weeks

Challenge

Google Authenticator is a dated application. Aside from key security features that other competitors have incorporated, they also lack brand cohesion and accessibility options.

Proposed Solution

After conducting article research and competitor analysis, I proposed a redesign of the Google Authenticator application to include accessibility options, modern security features, and better branding aesthetics.

Background

In 2015, there were 59 million cybersecurity incidents reported worldwide, and by 2020, this number rose to 300 million. Released in 2010, Google Authenticator is a Google application for two-factor authentication, commonly used in conjunction with Google accounts, and designed to reduce the risk of online breaches. Rather than verifying an account during the login process with a text message, Google Authenticator generates random codes a user can enter instead as the second factor.

Article Research

Each team member began by reading 3-4 articles to gain an objective understanding of Google Authenticator's affordances.

Competitor Analysis

We compared and contrasted the differences between Google Authenticator and other two-factor authenticator applications such as LastPass, Authy, and Duo. As part of this analysis, we also looked at User Reviews on the iOS app store to learn what types of features users liked or disliked about these applications.

User Personas

To facilitate our design process, we produced two user personas. Person A, a 30-year old active user of Google Authenticator, and Person B, a 51-year old first-time user of Google Authenticator. We sought to analyze the two broad categories of user types for Google Authenticator in order to improve upon the functional aspects of the application.

Ideation

Each team member began by creating 1-2 hand-drawn, low-fidelity sketches of a redesigned Google Authenticator based off of the background research we conducted.

Prototyping

Following ideation, my team reconvened and discussed the sketches we had created. We noted the features we felt were necessary, and agreed upon a sketch to create as a UI prototype in Figma.

Final Design

Using Figma, we created a clickable prototype of our redesigned version of Google Authenticator.

Takeaways

User personas were critical to understanding how to redesign this application. Each challenge presented me with an opportunity to be more empathetic in my decision-making as a designer.
